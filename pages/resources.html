Writing Strong Bills

Bill Drafting & Legislative Language

These documents walk you through how to write, structure, and language your bills so they read like real legislation—not just class assignments. Bring them to workshops, committee meetings, and floor debate prep.

Legislative Lingo (PDF)

A quick guide to the phrases, motions, and terminology you’ll hear in session and see on paper. Perfect for first-time delegates or anyone who wants to feel less lost in parliamentary jargon.

  • Core terms you’ll hear in committee and on the floor.
  • Plain-language explanations of common motions.
  • Useful reference to keep open during debate.

Before You Go to Session

Session Prep & Personal Toolkit

Beyond bill text, good preparation makes session way less stressful. Use this space as your checklist hub before traveling to statewide events or stepping into mock chambers on campus.

Personal Session Checklist

Use these as a starting point and add your own details based on your committee, campus, and role in the chapter.

  • Have your bill, notes, and speaking outline saved and backed up.
  • Review Legislative Lingo + Drafting Manual for last-minute clarity.
  • Confirm travel, housing, and dress code with the Exec Board.
  • Know your committee assignment and chair before you arrive.

For Committee & Floor Debate

A few habits that make your time on the mic—and off it—more effective.

  • Prepare a short 1–2 minute sponsor speech for your bill.
  • Write down 2–3 likely questions and your answers.
  • Have at least one friendly amendment idea if compromise is needed.
  • Bring a pen, notebook, and device charger. You’ll use them.
